How Small Businesses Can Keep A Track Of Field Employees? [6 Proven Methods]

Posted On:

24 Feb, 2024

-

Posted By:

Puja Singh

Tracking employees is the new buzzword now. Since employees are regarded as company assets, several employers want to keep an eye on them. Their primary purpose to monitor employees is to see whether they are being productive and useful to the company.

Does this apply to small and micro businesses? Well, businesses, irrespective of their stature should keep an eye on their employees to thrive more.

You must have come across larger corporations with employee tracking systems installed for their operations. Yes, they have the budget to purchase expensive employee tracking software. what about the small businesses?

Often, employees are sent to accomplish certain tasks on the field. How do you track them? How do you know if they are doing the task you’ve sent them for or got engaged in some other unproductive activities?

Well, there are some ways to track field employees and we’ll discuss them in this blog.

Why is it important to monitor the location of field employees?

Since field employees are always on the move, they tend to cheat at work and visit somewhere else. So, monitoring them becomes a need for employers. Tracking the location of employees helps to understand the following:

Because:

• The status of field employees while they are out for work. • The efficiency and productivity levels of the field employees.

Now, monitoring and tracking the location of field employees has become an easy and affordable job with MicroHR.

Methods to track field employees

Today, it’s important to track your employees, monitor them, and evaluate and improve their performance as per the business objectives. Wondering how to evaluate field employees? Here’s your answer:

1. Field employee monitoring software

Consider any business scenario. In every business world, time is money. If your business has field employees, it’s crucial to find out what your employees are doing, especially when they are outside the office premises. To help you in this regard, today, technology has come up with comprehensive field service management software for monitoring your field employees. Through this software, you can get a detailed report of the distance traveled, task progress, and performance analytics of employees.

2. Employee GPS tracker

Some companies employ people on a contractual basis. If your business has seasonal workers, tracking them through digital apps is definitely a complicated process. Further, monitoring them through video surveillance is yet again a hassle. Here, you should install GPS trackers and put them in sync with real-time employee tracking software. Through this tracker, you can get access to real-time information about the location of the employees along with their working hours.

3. Tools to monitor working hours

After COVID-19, the entire employment scenario has undergone a paradigm shift. The majority of the workforce is now choosing to stick to remote or hybrid working models for flexibility and convenience. Here, tracking employees can be a challenging task. However, monitoring the working hours of employees has become the new normal now. So, organizations now are looking for working hours monitoring tools to track the exact working hours of employees working from a distance.

4. SIM-based tracking

This is another feasible and effective method of tracking employees. You can use any featured phone or smartphone to track your employees. Here, the SIM card uses cellular towers to triangulate your employee’s device’s position. To be more precise, the tracker analyses the data of the sim card and provides accurate location information.

5. Network tracking

Any business organization wouldn’t want the unwanted intrusion of cyber attackers. So, an effective way of monitoring both outbound and inbound traffic is to track the network. Here, the tracking software makes use of firewall protection to check the network and content leaving and entering the company server.

6. Web monitoring

The web monitoring system is where the employees are asked to keep their tabs open to check the websites, social media sites, and applications the employees are using. Here, the employers can get comprehensive reports on employees’ tasks, and working hours.

• Small businesses have a tight budget. • Small businesses have less ecmployees. • Small businesses do not have technical experts to take care of complicated trackers.
